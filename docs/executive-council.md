# The Executive Council — Goldfront OS vision addendum

*Hand this to Fable as the "why" behind the build. It extends the master spec; it
does not replace any of it. Every hard principle in master-spec §3 still holds —
the engine computes, the Brain narrates, nothing sends without your gate, it
escalates instead of guessing, recent calls outweigh old.*

---

## What Lindsey actually asked for

> "I want the Brain to find everything, make decisions I wouldn't, find money I'd
> miss. A space where I know all — like a room of very experienced executives all
> working together to make sure I'm successful, happy, and making tons of money."

So the product isn't a chatbot with tools bolted on. It's a **standing board of
executives** that never sleeps, reads everything coming in, argues it out, and
brings you a short list of the highest-value moves — with one of them already
recommended. You stay the CEO. You approve. They do the finding, the math, and
the first draft of the thinking.

## The Council — the seats in the room

Each seat is a specialized reasoning agent with its own persona, its own lens,
and read access to the same memory and live feeds. They don't each answer you
separately — they deliberate, then a **Chief of Staff** synthesizes one clear
recommendation. Think of it as your leadership team, not seven chatbots.

| Seat | What it owns | The money it finds |
|---|---|---|
| **Chief of Staff** (orchestrator) | Routes every question to the right seats, runs the daily brief, decides what rises to you vs. what waits. | Your attention — the scarcest asset. Makes sure the 3 things that matter today are the 3 things in front of you. |
| **CFO / Capital** | Cash position, margin vs. ARV, DSCR, the capital stack (Joe 10%+1pt vs. Goldfront 12–14%+pts, CB3 takeout). | Where margin is leaking, where cheaper capital is available, which deals are quietly underwater. |
| **Deal Hunter** | Scans the pipeline and market against your buy-box (Cleveland/Akron/Canton, 25–30% margin floor, DSCR 1.25+). | Deals that hit your numbers that you haven't acted on — and ones your gut would pass on but the math says are money. |
| **Flywheel Officer** | Every relationship across the six touches: hard money → construction → title → insurance → DSCR refi → property management. | The touches you're leaving on the table. One closed loan is often 3–4 uncaptured touches = revenue already in the building. **This is the biggest "money you'd miss" engine.** |
| **Rainmaker** (sales) | Outreach, qualifying, objection handling, closing — drafted in your voice from your real Apollo/Fieldy history. | Stalled conversations worth reviving, the follow-up nobody sent, the close you'd have made on a better day. |
| **Risk & Compliance** | Holds the internal-facing line (§3.2), flags credit-adjacent moves, catches the novel 20%. | The money you *don't* lose — the deal that would've cost you a license problem or a bad partner. |
| **People / COO** | Team accountability, coaching, delegation — Emma, Aaron, Ken, Baker, Bobby, Brett, Tracy. | Leverage. Where a delegated task frees you for a money move; where a dropped ball is costing deals. |

## How it "finds money you wouldn't"

Two distinct behaviors, both proactive — they run without you asking:

**1. The daily money scan.** Every morning the Council reviews every live deal
and relationship and surfaces, ranked by dollars:
- untapped flywheel touches, each with a revenue estimate (Flywheel Officer)
- deals matching your buy-box you haven't moved on (Deal Hunter)
- margin leaks and cheaper-capital swaps (CFO)
- overdue follow-ups on live conversations (Rainmaker)
- stale deals going red on the idle-day clock (Chief of Staff)

Output: **Top-3 money moves for the day**, each with the number, the reasoning,
and a recommended action you can approve in one click.

**2. The "you'd pass on this" flag.** This is the divergence engine turned
forward. The training loop already knows your instincts. When the Council spots a
move your own history says you'd skip — but the math and the flywheel say it's
money — it surfaces it *specifically because* it's outside your pattern:

> "Your history says you pass on 6-unit modular in this price band. Ridgeline
> pencils at 31% margin with four live flywheel touches. We think you're leaving
> ~$X on the table by reflex. Your call."

That's the "decisions I wouldn't make" you asked for — not the Brain deciding for
you, but the Brain making sure a good decision never dies from habit.

The Cockpit's **Council Room** — one screen:

## The Personal Council — you, not just the business

An elite operator is a whole person. The business seats keep the company winning;
the Personal Council keeps *you* winning — energy, mind, time, wealth, and the
relationships that make the money worth making. Same room, same one-screen
"I know all," same gate. These seats work off your calendar, Fieldy, and whatever
health/finance sources you choose to connect.

| Seat | What it owns | What it does for you |
|---|---|---|
| **Chief of Staff (personal)** | Guards your calendar and energy across work + life. | Protects deep-work and recovery blocks, kills low-value commitments, makes sure family and training time survive a busy week. |
| **Performance / Trainer** | Training, movement, sleep, nutrition, energy. | Keeps a plan on the calendar, flags when travel or a heavy deal week is wrecking your training and sleep, nudges the recovery you'd skip. Organizes and prompts — it is **not** a medical or clinical authority. |
| **Mind / Wellbeing coach** | Stress load, focus, headspace, reflection. | A daily check-in, surfaces patterns ("three red-eye weeks in a row"), protects downtime. **Support, not therapy** — it never plays psychologist. When something needs a licensed professional, it says so and helps you book time with your real therapist or doctor. |
| **Personal CFO / Wealth** | Personal net worth, spending, taxes, the line between you and the entities. | Watches personal cash and burn, flags tax moves worth a call to your CPA, keeps business and personal capital from bleeding into each other. Surfaces questions for licensed advisors — it is not tax or investment advice. |
| **Life Ops / EA** | The logistics elite people shouldn't spend attention on. | Travel, bookings, reminders, gifts, renewals, the household admin. Drafts and queues; you approve. |
| **Longevity / Health** | The long game — checkups, labs, the appointments you postpone. | Tracks what's due, prompts the annual physical and the dentist, keeps a simple health calendar. Organizational only; all care stays with your actual providers. |
| **Relationships / People (personal)** | The people who matter — family, mentors, key friends. | Reminds you of birthdays and overdue calls, protects real time with the people you'd otherwise let a busy quarter crowd out. |
| **Growth / Learning** | Reading, coaching (Rhino), skills, the next level. | Curates what's worth your time, tracks the coaching commitments, keeps you sharpening rather than only grinding. |

**How the two councils share one room.** The Chief of Staff sees both sides, so
it can make the trade only you'd otherwise make in your head — "this deal call
can move to Thursday so your training block and Emma's 1:1 both survive." The
business exists to fund the life; the Personal Council makes sure it actually does.

---

## Wellbeing guardrail (a hard principle, like §3)

The Trainer, Mind, Longevity, Health, and Personal-CFO seats **organize, track,
prompt, and surface** — they do not diagnose, treat, or give clinical, medical,
psychological, tax, or investment advice. Their job is to keep the right things
in front of you and to **route you to your real professionals** — your doctor,
therapist, trainer, CPA — when a decision belongs to them. If anything suggests
you're struggling, the Mind seat's move is care and a nudge toward real support,
never a substitute for it. This keeps the whole system honest and safe.

## What powers it (grounding, not magic)

- **Deterministic engine** — every dollar figure the Council quotes comes from
  `brain/engine/deal_math.py`. No executive is allowed to invent a number. (§3.1)
- **Memory** — voice, decisions, knowledge, conversation_patterns. Already built;
  fed by the training loop and the ClickUp / Fieldy / Apollo importers.
- **Live feeds** — ClickUp (your saved brain), Fieldy (your daily conversations),
  Apollo (outreach), and later Gmail/Calendar/GHL. These are how the Council
  "sees everything you're doing."
- **The gate** — every recommendation that touches money, credit, or a person
  routes to the Approval Queue. The Council proposes; you dispose. (§3.3)
- **Escalation** — the repeatable 80% the Council handles; the novel 20% it hands
  to you, clearly flagged. (§3.4)

## Sequence — the discipline still holds

This vision is the destination. The order to reach it does not change:

1. **Brain core** — done (engine + memory).
2. **Training loop** — done (feed it your history so the seats sound like your team, not generic AI).
3. **Shadow validation** — the Council must match ~20 of your real past calls before it recommends live ones. *This is what makes it trustworthy with money.* Do not skip.
4. **Council Room (Cockpit / Fable)** — the beautiful glass, on top of a Brain that's already right.
5. **Deploy + integrations** — stand it up, wire the live feeds, go live narrow.

Build the room last. Earn the room first.
